Indonesian Cuisine

Indonesia is a Large Country located in South East Asia. With more than 17.000 islands, the cuisine in Indonesia diverse from one place to another. The cuisine will be divided based on the timezone that the cuisine originated from.

West Indonesia

Kerak telor

Kerak telor is a Betawi traditional spicy omelette dish in Indonesian cuisine. It is made from glutinous rice cooked with egg.

Mie aceh

Mie aceh is a noodle dish of Acehnese people from Aceh region, Indonesia. They are served in rich, hot and spicy curry-like soup.

Pempek

Pempek is a savoury Indonesian fishcake delicacy, made of fish and tapioca, from Palembang, South Sumatera, Indonesia.

Saksang

Saksang is a savory, spicy Indonesian dish from the Batak people.It is made from minced meat stewed in its blood, coconut milk and other spices.

Central Indonesia

Bebek betutu

Bebek betutu is a signature dish from Bali with duck as the main ingridient.

Patin baubar

Patin baubar is a traditional food from Borneo Island made from smoked catfish seasoned with a variety of spices.

Se'i

Se'i is a smoked, thinly sliced meat dish from the Timor people located in Nusa Tenggara Timur, Indonesia.

Sop konro

Konro soup is a rib soup originated from Sulawesi. The soup is brown-black in color with a strong and spicy taste from the mixture of spices used.

East Indonesia

Aunuve habre

Aunuve habre is a traditional food from Papua cooked by grilling seasoned skipjack fish with spices wrapped in taro leaves.

Gohu ikan

Gohu ikan is a sashimi-like food from Ternate made from raw fish. It is made from raw tuna or skipjack fish.

Ikan bungkus

Ikan bungkus is made from salt water fish seasoned with spices and wrapped in banana leaves and then grilled.

Papeda

Papeda is a traditional food with cornstarch as the primary ingridient. It has a similiar texture to glue and a plain taste.
